PROJECTS

Examining Realistic Animation for Bird Flight Locomotion and Flocking Behavior

An exploration into realistic and intelligent boids

This was a 5-person project for a graduate-level seminar, CS 275 - Artificial Life with Computer Vision and Computer Graphics led by professor Demetri Terzopoulos. Our motivation for this project was that life simulations focused on one aspect of artificial life--either locomotion, flock behavior, or sensory input--but not all at the same time!

Our project examines the flocking of birds given the ability to interact with the world around them. Our flocking behavior is based off of Craig Reynolds's Boids algorithm (intelligent flocking without collisions), with additional perching (with tiredness) and obstacle avoidance behaviors. Our birds have hunger and eat based on their hunger. In addition, they have the feeling of fear--when they see an enemy, they flee. This sensation of fear is incorporated based on simulated vision using computer vision (Tiny YoloV3). Birds also flee when they sense pheromones of fear from other birds, allowing for birds who have not seen the enemy directly to move with the flock.

For a more detailed look at our work, check out our presentation or our paper. Or, look at our video demo!
